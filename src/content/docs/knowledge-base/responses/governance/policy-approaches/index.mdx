## Overview

Beyond legislation, AI governance involves various **policy tools and regulatory mechanisms**. These approaches can be implemented through existing regulatory authority or voluntary adoption.

## Key Approaches

| Approach | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **[Compute Governance](/knowledge-base/responses/governance/policy-approaches/compute-governance/)** | Controlling AI development through compute access |
| **[Export Controls](/knowledge-base/responses/governance/policy-approaches/export-controls/)** | Restricting AI technology transfer internationally |
| **[Effectiveness Assessment](/knowledge-base/responses/governance/policy-approaches/effectiveness-assessment/)** | Evaluating what works in AI governance |

## Related Topics

For industry self-regulation approaches including RSPs and voluntary commitments, see [Industry Self-Regulation](/knowledge-base/responses/governance/industry/).

## Key Considerations

- **Enforcement mechanisms** — How policies are implemented and monitored
- **International coordination** — Avoiding regulatory arbitrage
- **Industry participation** — Role of voluntary vs. mandatory approaches
- **Adaptability** — Keeping pace with rapid AI development
